Thaha.

From public stone walls to real-time digital streams.

Decades before the internet connected people instantly, a message was already traveling across the hills of Nepal. In the 1970s, philosopher Rupchandra Bista shared a simple but powerful idea by writing one word across public walls and stones:

थाहा (To Know)

More than just a word, it represented a belief that awareness should reach everyone breaking the barrier between the unknown and the known. A message created for communities then continues today through technology, communication, and human connection.

Hulak (हुलाक) The traditional messenger system of Nepal, reborn as instant private communications.
Chautari (चौतारी) The traditional resting tree for public dialogue, reimagined as your digital room.
